Buying Process

While it's not legally required, working with a real estate agent offers significant advantages:
  • Expert guidance through the complex buying process
  • Access to market insights and property valuations
  • Negotiation support to get the best price
  • Assistance with paperwork and legal requirements
  • Identification of potential issues before they become problems
Our professional agents have deep market knowledge and are committed to finding your perfect property.

To purchase a property, you'll typically need:
  • Valid ID or passport
  • Proof of address (utility bills, bank statements)
  • Proof of income (pay stubs, tax returns)
  • Bank statements for the past 3-6 months
  • Pre-approval letter if using mortgage financing
  • Down payment funds verification
Additional documents may be required depending on your specific situation and the property type.

The property buying process typically takes 30-90 days from offer acceptance to closing. This timeline can vary based on:
  • Financing method (cash purchases are faster than mortgages)
  • Property inspection findings and required repairs
  • Title search complexity
  • Mortgage approval process
  • Local regulations and requirements
Our team works diligently to make the process as efficient as possible while ensuring all details are properly handled.

Renting Information

To qualify for a rental property, you'll typically need:
  • Completed rental application
  • Proof of income (usually 3x the monthly rent)
  • Credit check (minimum score requirements vary by property)
  • Background check
  • Previous landlord references
  • Security deposit (typically 1-2 months' rent)
  • First month's rent
Specific requirements may vary depending on the property owner and local regulations.

Modification policies vary by property:
  • Minor decorative changes (like hanging pictures with removable hooks) are usually allowed
  • Painting, installing fixtures, or making structural changes typically require written permission
  • Some properties have strict no-modification policies
  • Any approved modifications may need to be reversed when you move out
Always check your lease agreement and obtain written approval before making any significant changes to avoid potential penalties or deposit deductions.
